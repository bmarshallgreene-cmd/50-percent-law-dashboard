Full Methodology: H&W estimate = Employee Benefits (3000 series, ECS 84362 A Col 10) minus estimated STRS (19.1%) and Medicare (1.45%) contributions. Denominator = FON FT FTEF minus FT librarians. Calbright excluded (no FON entry). Data year: 2024-25.

Basic Methodology: H&W estimate = Employee Benefits (3000 series, ECS 84362 A Col 10) with no deductions. Denominator = raw FON FT FTEF with no librarian adjustment. This is a simpler calculation but will include STRS and Medicare employer contributions in the benefits figure, causing all values to be modestly overstated compared to Full Methodology. Useful as a quick reference or sanity check.

COL Index (Cost of Living): A relative cost-of-living index for each district's primary county, based on the MIT Living Wage Calculator's estimated living wage for one adult with no dependents (February 2026 data). The California statewide average is set to 100. A district with a COL Index of 83 is in a county roughly 17% below the state average cost of living; a district with 126 is about 26% above. Green values (<90) indicate lower-cost areas; red values (>115) indicate higher-cost areas. This index provides context for comparing H&W figures across regions — the same dollar amount provides meaningfully different purchasing power depending on where a district is located. Hover any COL Index cell to see the county name. Note: multi-campus districts are assigned to their primary or largest campus county.

Note on part-time faculty benefits: Districts that extend health & welfare benefits to part-time faculty will have higher 3000-series expenditures that are not fully attributable to full-time faculty. This may cause some districts' per-FT figures to be overstated relative to districts that offer benefits only to full-time employees.

* Self-insured districts: Districts marked with * operate self-insured or self-funded health plans where costs may not be fully captured in object code 3000 of the CCFS-311, causing figures to be understated.
  • Pasadena Area — Confirmed: routes H&W costs through an Internal Service Fund (Fund 61) and records them as operating expenses (object 5000), not employee benefits. Figure is significantly understated.
  • Shasta-Tehama-Trinity — Self-insured via STSIG JPA (Shasta Trinity Schools Insurance Group); claims administered by Anthem Blue Cross. CCFS-311 recording treatment unverified.
  • Riverside — Operates a standalone self-funded PPO administered by Health Now/Blue Shield. CCFS-311 recording treatment unverified.
Note: Many districts participate in SISC (Self-Insured Schools of California), a public JPA. SISC districts generally pay contributions that still appear correctly in object 3000 and are not flagged here. Districts using CalPERS health plans (e.g., Foothill-DeAnza, Los Angeles, North Orange County, San Mateo, Solano) are fully insured and not affected by this issue.
