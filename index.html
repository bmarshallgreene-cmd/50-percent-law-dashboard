Full Methodology: H&W estimate = Employee Benefits (3000 series, Col 10) minus estimated STRS (19.1%) and Medicare (1.45%) contributions. Benefits data is drawn from the Analysis of Compliance with the 50 Percent Law (ECS 84362) within the CCFS-311 Annual Report — the specific schedule that breaks expenditures out by program area, where Column 10 represents direct instructional programs only. Denominator = FON FT (Faculty Obligation Number full-time headcount) minus tenured/tenure-track FTEF in counseling and library roles, sourced from CCCCO Fall 2025 staffing data (ASA codes 6120, 6310, 6320, 6390, 6410, 6420, 6430). This adjustment removes non-instructional faculty from the denominator to better match the instructional-program-only benefits in the numerator. For eight districts reporting <5 FTEF (suppressed), 2.5 was used as a midpoint estimate. For eight additional districts where CCCCO data was zero or missing, counselor and librarian headcounts were manually researched from faculty directories, seniority lists, and the Council of Chief Librarians:
  • Antelope Valley — 19 counselors + 4 librarians (2024-25 college catalog)
  • Citrus — 21 counselors + 3 librarians (full-time faculty directory)
  • Gavilan — ~15 counselors + 2 librarians (2021 faculty roster; moderate confidence)
  • Lake Tahoe — 5 counselors + 2 librarians (faculty directory)
  • Redwoods — 5 counselors + 1 librarian (2024-25 faculty seniority list)
  • Solano — 7 counselors + 3 librarians (employee directory + CCLibrarians)
  • Copper Mountain — 8 counselors + 1 librarian (faculty directory + CCLibrarians)
  • Palo Verde — 7 counselors + 1 librarian (faculty directory + CCLibrarians)
Calbright excluded (no FON entry). Data year: 2024-25.

Note on instructional aides: Column 10 benefits include employer contributions for instructional aides (lab technicians, tutors, and other classified instructional support staff — Object 2200/2400), who are not counted in the FON FT denominator. This causes per-FT figures to be modestly overstated; the degree varies by district depending on the size of their instructional aide workforce.

Basic Methodology: H&W estimate = Employee Benefits (3000 series, ECS 84362 A Col 10) with no deductions. Denominator = raw FON FT with no counselor or librarian adjustment. This is a simpler calculation but will include STRS and Medicare employer contributions in the benefits figure, causing all values to be modestly overstated compared to Full Methodology. Useful as a quick reference or sanity check.

COL Index (Cost of Living): A relative cost-of-living index for each district's primary county, based on the MIT Living Wage Calculator's estimated living wage for one adult with no dependents (February 2026 data). The California statewide average is set to 100. A district with a COL Index of 83 is in a county roughly 17% below the state average cost of living; a district with 126 is about 26% above. Green values (<90) indicate lower-cost areas; red values (>115) indicate higher-cost areas. This index provides context for comparing H&W figures across regions — the same dollar amount provides meaningfully different purchasing power depending on where a district is located. Hover any COL Index cell to see the county name. Note: multi-campus districts are assigned to their primary or largest campus county.

Note on part-time faculty benefits: Districts that extend health & welfare benefits to part-time faculty will have higher 3000-series expenditures that are not fully attributable to full-time faculty. This may cause some districts' per-FT figures to be overstated relative to districts that offer benefits only to full-time employees.

* Self-insured districts: Districts marked with * operate self-insured or self-funded health plans where costs may not be fully captured in object code 3000 of the CCFS-311, causing figures to be understated.
  • Riverside — Operates a standalone self-funded PPO (district is its own risk-bearer, administered by Keenan/Blue Shield). Standalone self-funded plans are the most likely structure to route costs through an Internal Service Fund rather than object 3000; recording treatment unverified.
Note: Many districts participate in SISC (Self-Insured Schools of California), a public JPA. SISC districts generally pay contributions that still appear correctly in object 3000 and are not flagged here. Districts using CalPERS health plans (e.g., Foothill-DeAnza, Los Angeles, North Orange County, San Mateo, Solano) are fully insured and not affected by this issue. Shasta-Tehama-Trinity participates in STSIG JPA (Shasta Trinity Schools Insurance Group), an external JPA with the same contribution-based structure as SISC — costs are expected to flow correctly through object 3000 and are not flagged.

All 71 Districts view: Districts marked ✦ appear in the YFA survey and have all three methods — their Confidence column shows a 3-method conclusion using statewide (71-district) ranks for CCFS and ACCCA, with YFA scaled to the same statewide basis, so conclusions are consistent with the rank badges you see. All other rows show a 2-method CCFS+ACCCA conclusion (ranks out of 71). The scatter chart always shows all 71 districts (CCFS vs. ACCCA). Hover any Confidence cell for full rank detail.

 Confidence column logic — Labels step down from strongest to weakest: "Strong agreement: [quartile]" = all three methods place the district in the same quartile. "Moderate agreement: [quartile]" = two of three methods agree on the same quartile; one differs. "Both agree: [tier]" = two-method districts (CCFS + ACCCA only) where both methods agree. "Likely [tier]" = methods lean toward a tier but one is a clear outlier, or the two-method spread is moderate; check hover for detail. Tier labels: top/bottom quartile = all agreeing methods fall in Q1 or Q4; upper/middle/lower third = methods straddle a quartile boundary. "Leans top/bottom half" = methods spread too widely to name a quartile or third, but most point the same direction. "Unclear" (2-method only) = CCFS and ACCCA point to opposite halves. Click the Confidence header to sort from most to least confident. Hover any cell for individual rank detail. Known CCFS biases: self-funded ISF structures may understate CCFS for some districts (Pasadena, Riverside); instructional aide benefit bleed-in may overstate CCFS for others (West Kern, Lake Tahoe, Shasta-Tehama-Trinity).

Spearman rank correlation measures agreement in ordering — whether districts that rank high on one measure also rank high on another (1.0 = perfect agreement, 0 = no relationship). Current ρ values are shown in the subtitle above the table. YFA–ACCCA is typically the strongest pair because both are survey-based measures of actual per-employee contributions. CCFS correlates moderately with survey sources, reflecting that the formula-based approach captures additional structural factors (staffing ratios, part-time coverage, object-code allocation differences) beyond what surveys directly measure. Switching from YFA max to YFA avg medical narrows the 3-method pool to districts that reported avg data.
